Creating the Next Generation of Technologists

I mentor individuals and teams to grow into confident technologists, innovators, and problem-solvers. Through a mix of hands-on learning, project-based training, and leadership development, I help organizations build internal talent that understands creative technology, mechatronics, AI, and product management.

Core Areas of Teaching

Creative Technology

+
-

Teaching how to think creatively with technology — not just how to use tools. The focus is on choosing the right technology to express an artistic idea or feeling, whether through AR/VR, projection mapping, generative AI, or interactive installations. It’s about understanding how technology becomes a language for storytelling, emotion, and human connection.

Experiential Technology

+
-

Teaching how to design and apply technology in ways that unlock the senses and create unforgettable human experiences. This goes beyond screens and hardware — focusing on how sound, light, motion, scent, and interaction work together in campaigns, exhibitions, and spaces to build emotional connection. Students learn to think about experiences as journeys, where technology is a medium for memory-making, not a distraction.

Mechatronics

+
-

Teaching the integration of mechanics, electronics, and computing to bring ideas into motion. The focus is on how sensors, actuators, and control systems can transform concepts into interactive, kinetic, and responsive forms. Rather than treating mechatronics as pure engineering, I teach it as a creative toolkit — enabling students to prototype robots, installations, and intelligent objects that bridge imagination and reality.

Hands-On Training

+
-

Workshops, labs, and project-based exercises unlock real understanding — participants learn by doing, not just listening.

Activation Development

+
-

Teaching how to take a creative concept and transform it into a full-scale activation. The focus is on bridging the gap between idea and execution — from concept prototyping to production planning, resource mapping, and on-site delivery. Students learn how to align creative vision with technical feasibility, ensuring activations don’t just look good on paper but work flawlessly in real spaces.
